Explore KwaZulu-Natal


KwaZulu-Natal's coastline is dotted with small towns,many of which serve as recreational hubs. Beaches of world-class quality are to be found along virtually every part of South Africa's eastern seaboard, with some of the least developed gems found in the far southern and far northern ends of the province, Marina Beach was recognised as a Blue Flag beach in 2002.Most visitors come for the annual phenomenon on the KwaZulu-Natal coast of the 'Sardine Run'("the greatest shoal on earth")which occurs late Autumn or early Winter.
The intereior othe the province consists of largely rolling hills from the Valley of a Thousand Hills to the Midlands. These hills are grass covered,if there is no mist, you look down on one of the fairest valleys of Africa.This province also cotains rich areas of biodiversity of a arange of flora and fauna. The iSimangaliso Wetland Park and the UKhahlamba Drakensburg Park have been declared UNESCO World Heritage Sites.
